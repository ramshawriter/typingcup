The copyright symbol (©) marks who owns a creative work. Copy it with the button above, or type it yourself.

## Quick answer

On **Windows**, hold <kbd>Alt</kbd> and type <kbd>0169</kbd>. On **Mac**, press <kbd>Option</kbd> + <kbd>G</kbd>. In **Word**, type <kbd>(c)</kbd> and it auto-corrects to ©.

## Shortcuts at a glance

| Device / app | What to press |
| --- | --- |
| Windows | <kbd>Alt</kbd> + <kbd>0169</kbd> (numeric keypad) |

## On Windows and Mac

On **Windows**, hold <kbd>Alt</kbd>, type <kbd>0169</kbd> on the numeric keypad, release. On **Mac**, just press <kbd>Option</kbd> + <kbd>G</kbd> — quick and easy to remember.

## In Word and Google Docs

The easiest trick in **Word**: type <kbd>(c)</kbd> and keep typing — autocorrect turns it into ©. In **Google Docs**, the same <kbd>(c)</kbd> shortcut usually works, or use Insert → Special characters.

## FAQ

### Do I need the © symbol to hold copyright?
In most countries, copyright exists automatically the moment you create a work — the symbol is a notice, not a requirement. It's still useful for making ownership clear.
