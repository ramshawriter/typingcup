The fastest way to get a degree symbol is the copy button above — tap it and paste it wherever you need it. If you'd rather type it yourself, here's how on every device.

## Quick answer

On **Windows**, hold <kbd>Alt</kbd> and type <kbd>0176</kbd> on the number pad. On **Mac**, press <kbd>Option</kbd> + <kbd>Shift</kbd> + <kbd>8</kbd>. On **phones**, hold the <kbd>0</kbd> key (iPhone) or switch to symbols and long-press (Android).

## On Windows

Make sure Num Lock is on. Hold the <kbd>Alt</kbd> key, type <kbd>0176</kbd> on the **numeric keypad** (not the row of numbers above the letters), then release <kbd>Alt</kbd>. The degree symbol appears.

On a laptop without a number pad, the alt-code trick often won't work — use the copy button above, or the Windows emoji picker (<kbd>Win</kbd> + <kbd>.</kbd>) and search for "degree".

## On Mac

Press <kbd>Option</kbd> + <kbd>Shift</kbd> + <kbd>8</kbd> in any text field. That's it — the same shortcut works in almost every Mac app.

## On iPhone and Android

On **iPhone**, open the number keyboard, press and hold the <kbd>0</kbd> key, and a small ° will pop up to slide onto. On **Android**, switch to the symbols layout and long-press <kbd>0</kbd> the same way.

## In Word and Google Docs

In **Word**, type the code <kbd>00B0</kbd> and immediately press <kbd>Alt</kbd> + <kbd>X</kbd> — Word converts it to °. In **Google Docs**, go to Insert → Special characters and search for "degree".

## One thing to watch

The degree symbol (°) is **not** the same as the masculine ordinal indicator (º) used in Spanish, even though they look alike. For temperatures and angles, use ° — the one the copy button above gives you.

## FAQ

### What is the degree symbol used for?
Temperatures (25 °C), angles in geometry (90°), and map coordinates (40° N).

### Why doesn't Alt + 0176 work on my laptop?
Most laptops don't have a separate numeric keypad, which the alt-code needs. Use the copy button, the emoji picker, or turn on the on-screen number pad.
